In brief

I spent the first 10 years of my professional life studying rocks and simulating volcanoes and earthquakes 🌋. I then flipped my perspective and I am now building AI systems for satellite imagery 🛰. Our beautiful planet remains at the center of my interests 🌎.

Extended CV

I was first struck by the power of computer simulations during my studies in Civil Engineering at Politecnico di Torino. After completing the master, I enrolled for a PhD in Computational Mechanics at EPFL where I wrote a thesis about simulating damage (in FORTRAN 😱) in rocks to safely design nuclear waste repositories. After graduating from EPFL (and taking a 6 months break 🏖), I joined the group of Environmental Informatics at UFZ where I got the chance to sharpen my C++ skills by cooperating in the development of OpenGeoSys, an open-source framework for multi-physics simulations of porous media. Together with my colleagues, we researched deep geothermal systems by simulating its dynamics on one of the largest supercomputers on Earth, JUWELS.
